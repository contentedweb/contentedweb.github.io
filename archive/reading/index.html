Books I have read over time

The Outsiders

3 December, 2021

by S.E. Hinton

Easy to read book written when the author was a 16 year old in the 60s. Finished it in a week. Makes a change to the Iain M. Banks books I've read recently. Some quite insightful commentary on the pace of life of the rich not allowing them to anytime to actually enjoy life. Instead always running after more more more in the rat race. Once they have everything they need they still need to find something to run after. Also commentary on the poor never having anything but being more honest and down to earth, even if they are stealing and breaking the law. Otherwise a straightforward story with some violence, but not described in any real detail by today's standards.

Use of weapons

29 November, 2021

by Iain M. Banks

Very well written book with an interesting way of telling the story forward and backwards at the same time. I do find it helps to check my understanding of the author's books by reading online reviews and critiques, as I am otherwise missing details.

One second after

7 October, 2021

by William R. Forstchen

Easy to read story depicting how quickly our very civilized societies can be returned to the middle ages, feudal systems, sky high death rates and even cannibalism. A real page turner, which I finished over the course of a week. The back story is a little too "we're American, how could this happen", but the detail of what happens when anything electrical stops working is all too alarming. Highly recommended.

The player of games

30 September, 2021

by Iain M. Banks

Great book from one of the all time greats of science fiction. An Intergalactic game of Risk determines the fate of the main protagonists and the future of billions.

Blindsight

31 August, 2021

by Peter Watts

A well written hard science fiction book. The hard science can be difficult follow at times, but the concepts and storyline are original.

This is How You Lose the Time War

31 January, 2021

by Amal El-Mohtar and Max Gladstone

An interesting approach to cooperating on a book with each author "playing" one of the two spies chasing each other through time and space.

The Martian Chronicles

9 June, 2020

by Ray Bradbury

Sci-fi book about the colonisation of Mars by Earthlings! I like these sci-fi books from the 50s because even if the technology is all dated, the story and concepts are fresh and intriguing. Very nice story.

Farenheit 451

14 April, 2020

by Ray Bradbury

Story set in the future about an oppressive government that controls its citizens by burning all the books and keeping them entertained through television and social media like services. Somewhat prescient, given the current state of the media, especially social media and that the book was written in 1953.

Brave New World

10 February, 2020

Very interesting read and incredibly prescient for a book written in the 1930s. Well worth reading.

Handmaid's Tale

15 January, 2020

by Margaret Atwood

Good read, interesting alternate future scenario on the oppression of women by men.

One hundred years of soltitude

4 January, 2020

Fantastically written book about, quite a different style to other books.

Consider Phlebas

12 December, 2019

by Iain M. banks

Interesting read setting the scene for the other Culture novels, although there is no real continuity between the novels. It describes the galactic battles between the Culture and Idirans on a scale hard to imagine, but well brought to life in the novel. Also a good introduction to the authors style for writing science fiction. I find it hard to read, but the stories are interesting enough to make me want to continue. And let's not mention the names he comes up with!
